Your smiling face is requested on Saturday the 12th of January 2019 to attend our wedding. The ceremony will be held on the beach at 3.30pm in Little Akaloa, Banks Peninsula.

Not wanting to short-change ourselves, we’re actually hosting a whole weekend of festivities. On Friday afternoon we open the weekend with a German tradition called "Polterabend". This custom is meant to bring forth good luck and in addition, symbolically prepare us for the various trials that life has to offer (because we have to clean up the mess together).

Our reception will be a night of celebrating and dancing. To help heal the fragile on Sunday morning we are holding a brunch. To keep it German we will endeavour to squeeze as many condiments on the table as possible.

You are welcome to join us for as much or as little of the celebrations as you wish. We are looking forward to this weekend and we hope you can join us.

Little Akaloa, Banks Peninsula

How to get there

Located on the north eastern side of Banks Peninsula, Little Akaloa is approximately an hour and twenty minutes’ drive from Christchurch. The bay is accessed via Little Akaloa Road which descends from the Summit Road between Pigeon Bay and Okains Bay. We can gladly provide more help finding the place if you are unfamiliar with the bay. We will also help facilitate transport for those traveling from afar.

  • Plenty of parking along the waterfront
  • Waterfront camping ground true left of the creek
  • Note, there are shops nor cellphone coverage, however, both can be found with a shortish drive if need be.

Accommodation

We have secured an exclusive waterfront paddock for you to pitch a tent or set up your glamping station. There are toilet blocks and showers can be found if the ocean doesn’t cut the mustard.

If camping is not your scene we have secured a few baches. Most beds have already been allocated to those in need. If you’d prefer a bed to a camping mat let us know and we’ll see what we can do.
